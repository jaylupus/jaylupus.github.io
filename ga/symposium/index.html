Alcibiades

"I shall try in this way, men, to praise Socrates, through likenesses."

Agathon

"I declare that though all gods are happy, Eros (if sacred law allow it and it be without nemesis to say so) is the happiest of them, as he is the most beautiful and the best."

Eryximachus

"Now, there is one love that presides over the healthy state, and another over the sickly."

Socrates

"'It is at this place in life, in beholding the beautiful itself, my dear Socrates,' the Mantinean stranger said, 'that it is worth living, if--for a human being--it is [worth living] at any place.'"

Aristodemus

[He had been present at the party and, in my opinion, was the one most in love with Socrates at that time.]

Aristophanes

"So love is the name for the desire and pursuit of the whole."

Pausanias

"We all know that there is no Aphrodite without Eros; and were she one, Eros would be one; but since there are two Aphrodites, it is necessary that there be two Erotes as well."

Phaedrus

"So there is an agreement in many sources that Eros is among the oldest."
